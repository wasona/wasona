## How to say deer without a word for deer

Interestingly, English and Toki Pona don't match up precisely! A deer and a sparrow in English become "animal" and "bird" in Toki Pona. Why is that?

We only keep the details that matter to get the idea across. Our story wouldn't change if soweli Mumu was a cow, or a squirrel, or a monkey. The reader imagines any animal they want. We do the same in English a little bit: you can imagine waso Siju's feathers in any colour, because it's not important to the story.

What if we continue the story and it becomes important? Then new details will make the picture clearer. Maybe later in the story, waso Siju flies away -- now we know he's not an ostrich. Maybe soweli Mumu's dad has antlers -- now we know she's not a squirrel.

We paint the picture one new detail at a time, until it's specific enough. This way, our 130ish words can come together to describe anything. You already know 40 of them, so keep going!
